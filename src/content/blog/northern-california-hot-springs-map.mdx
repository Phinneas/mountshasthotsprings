<h2 id="frequently-asked-questions">Frequently Asked Questions </h2><p><strong>How many hot springs are in Northern California?</strong> Northern California has over 30 documented natural hot springs, ranging from developed resort facilities to primitive wilderness pools accessible only by hiking. </p><p><strong>Can you camp at Northern California hot springs?</strong> Yes! Several locations offer camping including Sierra Hot Springs (on-site camping), Harbin Hot Springs (various accommodation levels), and Hunt Hot Springs (nearby forest service camping areas). </p><p><strong>What are the best free hot springs in Northern California?</strong> Hunt Hot Springs offers free access to the springs themselves (forest service parking fees may apply). Most developed resort springs charge day-use fees ranging from $20-40.</p><p><strong>Do I need reservations for Northern California hot springs?</strong> Reservations are recommended for popular springs like Harbin and Sierra Hot Springs, especially on weekends and during peak winter season. Day-use reservations are often required. </p><p><strong>Are Northern California hot springs safe?</strong> When proper precautions are followed, hot springs are generally safe. Always test water temperature, stay hydrated, limit soaking time, and be aware of your surroundings, especially at remote locations like Hunt Hot Springs.</p>
